LEAD's Internship Project

An overview of the interning idea here at LEAD

The LEAD internship class would be like Design Thinking and Emerging LEADers, a LEAD exclusive class that allows students to go into their particular career interests. This will help LEAD get even more of a leading edge as it's only available to freshman and sophomores at Park Hill.

This can be very helpful to just about everyone who uses it. If students get into the real work environment it can help them get insight on their job, as well as introduce them to new career types and possibilities. Having internships at LEAD just seems natural, LEAD innovation studio is well, innovative. So having something like interning would be the innovative way to help students understand what workplaces and careers are REALLY like. Students can also get paid for internships. According to Glassdoor the average salary for a high school intern is 12$ hourly, of course, it depends on the job and other variables but it's still more than minimum wage and connecting with professionals in professional spaces... As a teenager. In fact, there are so skills you gain or at least begin to understand/learn...

  • Adaptability - Employers like people and look for those who can adapt to changes, this is a life skill in general as well as in our lives we experience many changes whether that be just this weekend when I moved to a new house instead of an apartment, or there is a new student at our school. Adaptation is very much a necessity for human expansion and success in every aspect of life.
  • Respect - When I say respect I don't mean like going all Japanese feudalism style and going all formal all the time but more like being able to understand and respect others opinions. When you are working professionally with a group it is vital that you are able to communicate on a professional level. If you allow your differences get the best of your work, you are destined to fail or not reach your projects maximum potential.
  • Communication - Communication is essential and really there isn't much need for me to talk about it, being able to have a strong knowledgeable claim and know what you are talking while accepting other viewpoints is a virtue that I can very strongly say will help us. This ability is practiced especially in internships and workplaces. When talking to an adult in your career who is extremely experienced you learn a lot, but being able to show your competence in the industry as just a student is very formidable. Most adults will not think the highest of you so it isn't hard if you really try to astonish them.
